Problem

The Game Jam's theme was "Built to Scale". We took that quite literally and made a tower defense game where you build the towers and apply upgrades to scale them. While conceptually straightforward, implementing a scalable tower defense system within a 96-hour time constraint presented significant architectural and coordination challenges.

My Job

My role in the project was to design and implement the foundational architecture that the rest of the team built upon. I developed the stage system, including dynamic stage loading, enemy spawning, and tile interaction mechanics, ensuring that other team members had the tools and interfaces necessary to implement their features. I also served as the primary integration lead, making sure individual scripts functioned cohesively within the core architecture. When necessary, I refactored core systems and built additional tooling to support feature development and maintain cross-system compatibility.

Additionally, I worked closely with the lead frontend developer to refine the overall game vision and ensure that the underlying systems supported the gameplay and UI requirements needed for implementation.

Reflections

  • Version control is critical in collaborative development and must be managed carefully to prevent integration issues.
  • Larger teams introduce coordination complexity, requiring clear communication and a shared understanding of system requirements.
  • Time constraints significantly impact development quality; proactive planning and task delegation help mitigate last-minute bottlenecks.
